Frequently Asked Questions

Solar energy is energy from the sun that is converted into thermal or electrical energy. Using solar panels, this sunlight can be converted into usable electricity for homes, businesses, and other applications.
Solar panels use photovoltaic (PV) cells to absorb sunlight and convert it into direct current (DC) electricity. An inverter then converts DC into alternating current (AC) for use in homes or businesses.
Solar reduces your electricity bills, protects you from rising energy costs, and often qualifies for government incentives and tax credits.
The cost varies depending on system size, location, and installation.
Solar panels still generate electricity during cloudy days, though at reduced efficiency. At night, they don’t produce power. To maintain power, you draw from the grid or use a battery storage system.
Once permits are approved, most residential systems take 1 to 3 days to install. The total process including design, permitting, and inspection may take 4–8 weeks.
Solar systems require very little maintenance. Occasional cleaning and periodic system checks (often done remotely) are enough. Most systems are monitored 24/7 for performance.

Solar energy is clean, renewable, and reduces greenhouse gas emissions. By switching to solar, you help combat climate change and reduce air pollution.
If you don’t have batteries, your solar system will shut off during a power outage for safety reasons (unless you have a battery backup or off-grid system). With batteries, you can continue to power essential appliances.
